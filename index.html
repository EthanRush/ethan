Dance Dance Revolution Children's Museum Exhibit

For this concept project my team created a game inspired by Dance Dance Revolution that could be used to teach children about piezoelectricity and its relation with kinetic energy. Piezoelectricity is created through the conversion of kinetic energy into electricity. Much cleaner than fossil fuels, this process has been famously used in piezoelectric generator pads under sidewalks that collect energy from footsteps. My team made wooden dance floor tiles that connected to a computer game. When the user follows the game and steps on the right tiles at the right time, a 3D printed shape lights up green. When they step on the wrong tile or move too slowly, the shape lights up red and the game ends. When the user ends the game, the ending screen tells the user how much energy they could have produced with their movement, and shows how long that energy could have powered a lightbulb. In order to build this project, my team had to make use of both MatLab for the software side of the game and Arduino for our hardware inputs and outputs. To take in these inputs, we had to construct several circuits by connecting wires to sheets of tinfoil, allowing a signal to go through only when the pads were stepped on and the tinfoil collides.

Simulink Robot Arm

For this project my partner and I created a controlable robot arm that, through the use of the Zedboard’s switches and push buttons, could move any or all of its 5 joints in a 0-180 degree motion. In order to design the robot’s behavior, we made use of Simulink through MatLab, designing logical circuits to conduct and control the movements of the arm. Upon completion we were able to successfully use the robotic arm to conduct several simple tasks: from picking up an eraser and putting it back in the box to making the arm push its own Off switch.

RedBoard Box

For this project I designed a wooden casing to hold a RedBoard circuit. The casing had to not only be aesthetically pleasing, but also fit the RedBoard perfectly while allowing easy access to its ports and power button. I designed the casing using AUTOCAD and created the boxes parts, as well as its engraved designs, using a laser cutter.
