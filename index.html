Cat's Cradle and Death of a Salesman


I formatted the novel Cat's Cradle by Kurt Vonnegut Jr. and the play Death of a Salesman by Arthur Miller.   After designing both books as a series, I had them published in one volume with coordinating covers.

I set Cat's Cradle in the typeface Mrs. Eaves OT.   Designed to to fit 5x8 inch paper, the text is at 10 pt with a 12 pt leading.   The only treatment given to the chapter title pages, other than its margins, were the small caps used for the chapter titles.

After realizing how difficult the first version was to read, I expanded the paper size to 6x9 inches, and the text to 11 pt.   I also made the running heads in small caps.   In the chapter title pages, I added a small caps treatment to the first three words of the chapter.   To differenciate it from the chapter title, I changed the tracking to 200.

In the final version of Cat's Cradle, I edited the overall look of the text by adjusting the size to 12 pt (with 14 pt leading) and the tracking of the body text to -10.   In the chapter title pages, I expanded the small caps treatment to include the entire first line and changed the tracking of it to match the rest of the text.   I made the chapter titles have 200 tracking instead.

I set Death of a Salesman in the typeface Adobe Text Pro.   Designed to fit 6x9 inch paper, the text is at 11 pt with 13.2 pt leading.   I set the running heads as 11 pt font also, but differentiated it from the text by putting it in italics.

In the final version of Death of a Salesman, I adjusted the tabs to make the character names closer to the main text.   I also decreased the size of the running heads to 9 pt, in order to better differentiate it from the remainder of the text.

Book cover Series

After formatting Cat's Cradle and Death of a Salesman, I made typographic book covers for them.   I had two objectives: the covers had to be only made up of type, and the two covers had to work together as part of a series.

I began working on the book covers by brainstorming different ideas for Cat's Cradle and Death of a Salesman.   These were a few ideas that I had, but, among other problems, neither of them could translate into a series of covers.

My next iteration of book covers were clearly part of a series, but were not very interesting or worth revising.   I decided to try again with a new idea.

My next idea for the book covers was more sound and translated well into a series.   I decided to take this idea and revise it until it was more successful.

I printed out the two covers, traced them by hand, then scanned my tracings to translate them into vectors.   At this point the only step left was to add color, leaving me with the final version of the covers below.

Broadside Poster

My goal for this project was to make a poster that showcased a specific typeface on an A2 poster in a way that was both simple and visually interesting.

The first poster I made, while showing a clear hierarchy, was not very visually interesting and had little tension.

The second poster that I made had more tension, but contained a few elements that were unnecessary.   I also realized that the poster could benefit from a better color choice.

In order to make the final poster, I simplified the overall layout and allowed the hgihlighted letterform to become much larger.   I also switched out the darker red for a brighter yellow.

The Nursery Alice

I worked on a picture book by formatting The Nursery Alice (the abridged version of Alice in Wonderland) by Lewis Carroll and illustrated by John Tenniel.   Later I printed out the book and bound it using saddle stitching.

I set the book in Bodoni 72, with 10 pt font and 12 pt leading.

After doing the initial formatting, I began adding images.   I decided to use John Tenniel's linear drawings rather than his color illustrations.

In the final version I increased the text size to 11 pt with a 14 pt leading to increase readability.   I removed the running heads from the chapter titles, and I added color, using purple for the chapter titles and gold for the illustrations.

After formatting The Nursery Alice, I made a book cover for it.

I decided that the book cover should include an image of John Tenniel's famous white rabbit.   I set the title in the typeface Mrs. Eaves OT, put the author's name in small caps, and semi-aligned the ragged edge of the text to the form of the rabbit.

Next I played around with color.   I decided to pick a dark background with white text that would match the white rabbit.

After adding the dark background I noticed that the background of the illustration looked off, so using Photoshop I edited it out, leaving just the rabbit and the text.
